ASTRONAUTS

The brave men and women who dared to leave our world

Yuri Gagarin

🇷🇺 Soviet Union

The first human in space. On April 12, 1961, Gagarin orbited Earth once aboard Vostok 1 in a 108-minute flight that changed history forever.

Neil Armstrong

🇺🇸 United States

Commander of Apollo 11 and the first person to walk on the Moon on July 20, 1969. His words — "one giant leap for mankind" — echoed around the world.

Valentina Tereshkova

🇷🇺 Soviet Union

The first woman in space, flying solo aboard Vostok 6 in June 1963. She completed 48 orbits of Earth over nearly three days.

Sally Ride

🇺🇸 United States

The first American woman in space, flying on the Space Shuttle Challenger in 1983. She became an inspiration for generations of future scientists.

Chris Hadfield

🇨🇦 Canada

Commander of the ISS and famous for sharing life in space with the world through social media and a viral performance of Space Oddity in orbit.

Peggy Whitson

🇺🇸 United States

Record-holder for the most cumulative time spent in space by any American. She has lived aboard the ISS on multiple long-duration missions.

Sunita Williams

🇺🇸 United States

Known for completing a triathlon from space and holding records for spacewalk time by a female astronaut. A pioneer of modern human spaceflight.

Buzz Aldrin

🇺🇸 United States

Lunar Module Pilot on Apollo 11 and the second human to walk on the Moon. A lifelong advocate for Mars exploration and space travel.
